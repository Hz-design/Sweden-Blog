The exposure triangel and controlling the camera.

Color space
  • RGB digital screen format
  • Adobe RGB more colour space, more color information! Use this every camera has this setting
HDR
  • Dynamic range, a photo with a high dynamic range (HDR) allows greater flexibility in editing.
White Balance
  • choosing a white balance that best matches your light source, so that it will looks white, not yellow or blue.
Raw and JPeg
  • Raw: non lossy + high dynamic range
  • Jpeg: lossy + low dynamic range
Lens
  • Lenses vary in focal length, measured in millimeters.
  • Focal length dramatically affects the appearance of your subject
  • 8mm - distorted fish eye effect
  • 35mm - standard without distortion

The Exposure Triangle

  • the 3 most important things that affect your image quality.
  • Iso, aperture and Shutter speed
  • Your camera should have an exposure level indicator.
  • 0 doesn't always mean right, strong light sources influences the exposure meter and get give you the user wrong settings, it really depends what you eant to achieve
Histogram
  • Darks
  • Shadows
  • Mid Tones
  • White
  • Highlights
Aperture
  • Aperture is the amount of light let in. Aperture is measured in f-stops.
  • F2 large aperture
  • Aperture controls the depth of field (DOF)
  • A large aperture such as f1.4 would result in a shallow depth of field.
  • A small Aperture such as f22 would result in Deep Focus
  • Proximity also counts

ISO

  • ISO(international Standards Organisation) = Sensitivity level
  • Analogue film used fixed ISO values, digital is interchangeable
  • The higher the ISO: the greater the noise
  • Noise problems look at dark places and edges

Shutterspeed

  • shutter speed affects the amount of time an image is exposed.
  • Shutter speed is the exposure duration measured in seconds.
  • The more available light the more shutter speed you can have

Software

  • Adobe Lightroom, professional photography,
  • Photshop, graphic design, photography,
  • Adobe Bridge, categorizing and rating for yourself

What is Location Photography?

  • It is when you go to where the subject is instead of the subject coming to you

Location Lightning

  • Essential aspect of photography and videography.
  • Illuminating a scene or sebject
  • Desired mood or atmosphere

Lightning types

  • Natural lights, sun, moon other natural environment sources
  • Artificial lights, man made sources, light bulbs, flash units, LED
  • Combination of the two

Location Lightning Techniques

  • Reflectors, are used to reflext to light to a subject
  • Diffusers, soften the light and reduce half shadows
  • Light modifiers, shape and control the light such as softboxes and umbrellas

Location lighting equipment

  • Flash units
  • Led lights
  • Tripods and stands
  • Light Meters, used to measure the intensity of light in a scene.
  • Plan, prepare, execute a location shoot
  • Identify location lightning challenges and solutions
